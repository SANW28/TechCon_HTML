History:Origin and Evolution of TechCon

TechCon began in 2010 as a small tech gathering in Accra, Ghana, and has since grown into a premier regional conference. Initially focused on software, it expanded to include hardware, cybersecurity, and AI by 2015. Today, TechCon attracts thousands of attendees, featuring keynotes, workshops, and an expo, fostering innovation and collaboration in Ghana's tech ecosystem.

Mission

TechCon is a leading tech conference in Ghana, dedicated to fostering innovation, collaboration, and education. It showcases the latest technology trends through workshops and sessions, facilitates partnerships among industry leaders, and empowers attendees with valuable skills. Emphasizing inclusivity and ethical tech use, TechCon aims to address societal challenges and promote sustainable development in the region.

Past Speakers

Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala

  • Biography: Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala is a Nigerian economist and international development expert. She served as the Managing Director of the World Bank and Finance Minister of Nigeria. She has been a prominent advocate for economic reform and development in Africa.
  • Contributions: Ngozi's leadership and advocacy for global economic reforms have had a profound impact on Africa's economic policies and development strategies

Patrick Awuah

  • Biography: Patrick Awuah is a Ghanaian engineer and educator, known for founding Ashesi University, a leading institution in Ghana focused on innovation and entrepreneurship. He is a recipient of the MacArthur Fellowship.
  • Contributions: Patrick's work at TechCon has centered on education reform and fostering a new generation of ethical leaders and entrepreneurs in Africa through Ashesi University.

Rebecca Enonchong

  • Biography: Rebecca Enonchong is a Cameroonian entrepreneur, technology advocate, and founder of AppsTech. She is a leading voice for women in technology in Africa and serves on several boards promoting entrepreneurship and tech innovation.
  • Contributions: Rebecca's initiatives at TechCon have focused on empowering African entrepreneurs and promoting digital literacy, advancing the role of women in technology across the continent
